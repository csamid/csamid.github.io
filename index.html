California State University, Long Beach
Hand Grasp Assessment Grip-Load Interface
Synchronous coordination of grip force (G) and load force and load force (L) is needed to prevent object slippage or breakage. As a NIH BUILD Scholar, I redesigned an interface to measure G and L for evaluation and rehabilitative training of individuals with hand impairment, such as stroke survivors. The redesigned interface consists of a device acting as the object to manipulate, a brace for wrist adjustability, and a display for visual feedback to the user.
California State University, Long Beach
Scuffer
For my senior capstone project my team developed Scuffer, a low-cost quadruped. Aimed at lowering the entry to engage in robotics for future CSULB students, Scuffer is centered around a Arduino Micro with a 3D printed structure. As the control systems lead, I taught the basics of Arduino coding, facilitated group literature reviews, and lead all control integration.
University of Michigan
Leg 2 Design Optimization
Leg 2 is a powered knee-ankle prosthetic leg from the Locomotor Control Systems Lab at UM. For the Summer of 2021, I optimized the design of Leg 2 for experiments with a new continuous phase-varying impedance controller. This involved redesigning various 3d printed parts and the remote control of a treadmill and mocap software.
California State University, Long Beach
Hand Grasp Assessment Knob Device
Hand Impaired individuals struggle with activities of daily living, such as opening a jar. We developed the knob device to measure hand forces and torques for evaluation and rehabilitative training. I worked on integrating force-sensing resistors (FSRs), a torque sensor, and a motor for varying haptic feedback.
